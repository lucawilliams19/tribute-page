Alan Turing

Turing c. 1928 at age 16

Alan Turing was a mathamatician during the second world war. He helped the allies defeat the germans by working on a top secret war effort to decrypt German navel communications. His work shortend the war by more than 2 years. He saved an estimated 14 million lives. He was never given recognition during his time serving, this was partially due to the secret nature of the project and also do to the rampant homophobia that still plagues modern day interactions. Many people didn't know who Alan Turing was until the movie "The Immitation Game".
